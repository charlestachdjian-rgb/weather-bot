Why Both Losing Days Look the Same

Both Feb 15 and Feb 18 share an unusual pattern: the temperature peaked late at night, not in the afternoon.

Normally in February, the daily high occurs between 12pm-4pm as the sun heats the ground. But on these two days, a warm air mass arrived in the evening, pushing temperatures up after sunset. This broke two assumptions:

  • Ceiling NO (4pm): assumed the temperature wouldn't climb 2¬∞C+ more after 4pm
  • Locked-In YES (5pm): assumed the running high at 5pm was the final daily high

On both days, the temperature surged +4¬∞C to +6¬∞C after 5pm. This is rare but clearly happens in Paris winter.

Takeaway: What's Safe, What's Risky

  • Floor NO ‚Äî Tier 1: Zero losses, ever. Once the running high passes a threshold, it can never go back. This is a mathematical certainty. The edge per trade is small (pennies) but there is no risk.
  • Floor NO ‚Äî Tier 2: Zero losses in our data. Would only lose if the forecast is wrong by 4¬∞C+. Very unlikely but not impossible.
  • Ceiling NO: Lost 2 times. The losses were tiny ($0.05) only because the market had already priced YES near 100%. If the market is unsure (YES at 30-50%), a wrong Ceiling NO could lose $50-$70 per $100 bet. The 4pm cutoff is too early for Paris winter.
  • Locked-In YES: Lost 2 times. The losses were small ($0.75-$2.45) only because YES was cheap. If we'd bought YES at 50%, we'd have lost $50. The 5pm cutoff is far too early ‚Äî temperature surged +4-6¬∞C after 5pm on both losing days.

Recommendation: Only use Floor NO (Tier 1 + Tier 2) for real money. Ceiling NO and Locked-In YES could work with much later cutoff times (10pm+) or with additional safeguards (declining temperature trend confirmed by SYNOP), but the current parameters are unsafe.
